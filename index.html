💡 Prediabetes Tips
🥦
Cook at home more
Home-cooked meals let you control ingredients and sugar. Aim for groceries to be 70%+ of your food budget.
🩸
Watch hidden sugars dining out
Restaurant meals often have 2–3× more sugar than home cooking. Use your dining budget as a conscious limit.
☕
Coffee drinks add up fast
Flavored lattes can have 40–70g of sugar. Track them separately and opt for unsweetened options.
📊
Your budget is a health tool
A lower dining-out budget naturally pushes you toward home cooking — one of the best changes for prediabetes.

Zero-Based Budgeting
Give every dollar a job.
Income minus all allocations equals exactly zero — not because you're broke, but because every dollar has been intentionally assigned a purpose.
"A budget is telling your money where to go instead of wondering where it went."
— Dave Ramsey
The 5-Step Method
1
Enter your total income
Every dollar coming in this month — salary, freelance, side hustles, everything.
2
Pay yourself first
Before bills, before fun — allocate to savings first. Your future self is the most important payee.
3
Assign every remaining dollar
Go through every category until "Left to Allocate" shows $0.00.
4
Track actual spending
Log each expense as you go. Compare actuals to your plan — this is where habits shift.
5
Reset & refine each month
Build a fresh budget every month. Each cycle you'll get more accurate.
Core Principles
🎯
Zero doesn't mean broke
Every dollar has a destination. You're not left with nothing — you're left with a plan.
💰
Savings is a mandatory expense
Budget for savings first, spend the rest after. It's not what's left over — it's line item #1.
🔄
Every month starts fresh
Build a new budget based on that month's actual income. Realistic and always current.
📊
Awareness changes everything
Most overspending happens on autopilot. Tracking each expense builds the awareness that rewires habits.
🌱
Progress over perfection
Your first budget won't be perfect. Each month you'll get more accurate and more confident.
✨
Intention, not restriction
It's about consciously choosing where your money goes — including guilt-free joy spending.
